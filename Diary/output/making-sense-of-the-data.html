Making sense of the data

04 agosto 2016

I have new information on the data ! The input signal \(u\) is more than just a percentage. It is the difference in throttle between the front and the rear motor expressed in percentages.

Written simply it is:

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} u &= (Throttle Front)_{\%} - (Throttle Rear)_{\%} \\ &= \Omega_{F\%} - \Omega_{R\%} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

If we recall the conventions used in the previous work then:

\begin{equation*} \begin{alignedat}{4} \delta \Omega_F &&= \Omega_F - \Omega_H \quad &&\Rightarrow&& \quad \Omega_F &&= \delta \Omega_F + \Omega_H\\ \delta \Omega_R &&= \Omega_R - \Omega_H \quad &&\Rightarrow&& \quad \Omega_R &&= \delta \Omega_R + \Omega_H\\ \end{alignedat} \end{equation*}

The control scheme imposes that \(\delta \Omega_F = -\delta \Omega_R = \delta \Omega\). By substituting this information into the previous results we arrive at:

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \Omega_F &= \delta \Omega + \Omega_H \\ \Omega_R &= -\delta \Omega + \Omega_H \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Which allows us to rewrite the expression of the input \(u\) as:

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} u &= (\delta \Omega + \Omega_H)_{\%} - (-\delta \Omega + \Omega_H)_{\%} \\ &= 2 \cdot \delta \Omega_{\%} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

However this doesn't bring us any closer to calculating the expression of \(\delta \Omega_{\%}\). Assuming that:

\begin{equation*} \delta \Omega_{\%} = \dfrac{\delta \Omega}{\delta \Omega_{Max}} \cdot 100 \end{equation*}

Then we still need the value of \(\delta \Omega_{Max}\) to make any headway.
