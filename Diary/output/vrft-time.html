VRFT Time

05 maggio 2016

Today I need to figure out why the VRFT controller is sooo fucked up ...

Expanding a PID

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} G(s) &= K_p + K_i \frac{1}{s} + \frac{K_ds}{1 + sT_f} \\ &= \frac{K_p s + K_i}{s} + \frac{K_d s}{1 + sT_f} \\ &= \frac{k_p s (1 + s T_f) + K_i (1 + s T_f) + Kd s^2}{s (1 + s T_f)} \\ &= \frac{k_p s + K_p T_f s^2 + K_i + K_i T_f s + K_d s^2}{s^2 T_f + s} \\ &= \frac{(K_p T_f + K_d) s^2 + (K_p + K_i T_f) s + K_i}{s^2 T_f + s} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Expanding a discrete time PID

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} G(s) &= k_p + K_i \frac{T_s}{z - 1} + K_d \frac{1}{T_f + \frac{T_s}{z - 1}} \\ &= k_p + K_i \frac{T_s}{z - 1} + K_d \frac{1}{T_f + \frac{T_s}{z - 1}} \\ &= \frac{K_p (z - 1) + K_i T_s}{z - 1} + K_d \frac{z - 1}{T_f (z - 1) + T_s} \\ &= \frac{\left( K_p z - K_p + K_i T_s \right) \left( T_f (z - 1) + T_s \right) + K_d (z - 1)^2}{(z - 1) \left( T_f (z - 1) + T_s\right)} \\ &= \frac{(K_p T_f z^2 - K_p T_f z + K_p T_s z) (-K_p T_f z + K_p T_f - K_p T_s) (K_i T_s T_f z - K_i T_s T_f + K_i T_s^2)(K_d z^2 - 2 K_d z + K_d)} {T_f z^2 +(T_s - 2 T_F) z + (T_f - T_s)} \\ &= \frac{(K_p T_f + K_d) z^2 + (-2 K_p T_f + K_p T_s + K_i T_s T_f - 2 K_d) z + (K_p T_f - K_p T_s - K_i T_s T_f + K_i T_s ^2 +K_d)} {T_f z^2 + (T_s - 2 T_f) z + (T_f - T_s)} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

We can isolate the different terms of the equation such that:

\begin{equation*} G(s) = \frac{a_2 z^2 + a_1 z + a_0}{b_2 z^2 + b_1 z + b_0} \end{equation*}

Comparing the two equations we have:

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} a_2 &= K_p T_f + K_d \\ a_1 &= -2 K_p T_f + K_p T_s + K_i T_s T_f - 2 K_d \\ a_0 &= K_p T_f - K_p T_s - K_i T_s T_f + K_i T_s ^2 +K_d \\ b_2 &= T_f \\ b_1 &= T_s - 2 T_f \\ b_0 &= T_f - T_s \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

In the case where \(T_f = T_s = T\) the equations reduce to:

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} a_2 &= K_p T_f + K_d \\ a_1 &= - K_p T + K_i T^2 - 2 K_d \\ a_0 &= K_d \\ b_2 &= T \\ b_1 &= - T \\ b_0 &= 0 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

This provides a hint as to why the controller obtained through VRFT has a correct denominator. The denominator is not affected by the PID parameters but by the fixed time constant and sampling interval.

If only I knew why. Compare the OptimalInnerController, the PID produced by the VRFT method and the original PID controller:

>> tf(c2d(Ri, Ts))

ans =

  0.562 z^2 - 1.076 z + 0.5245
  ----------------------------

Discrete-time transfer function.

ans =

  24.02 z^2 - 44.3 z + 20.94
  --------------------------
    z^2 - 1.368 z + 0.3679

Discrete-time transfer function.

For reference purposes we compare a discretised continuous time PID with an actual discrete time PID:

>> tf(c2d(pid(1, 1, 1, 1), 1))

ans =

   2 z^2 - 2.368 z + 1
  ----------------------
  z^2 - 1.368 z + 0.3679

Sample time: 1 seconds
Discrete-time transfer function.

>> tf(pid(1, 1, 1, 1, 1))

ans =

  2 z^2 - 2 z + 1
  ---------------
      z^2 - z

Sample time: 1 seconds
Discrete-time transfer function.

It is immediately obvious that even though the numerators are slightly different they are similar. The output of the VRFT is completely fucked up.

Turns out that the root of the issue is that I forgot to take the Mixer into account when trying to run the VRFT. After fixing that everything went swimmingly !

Next step: the outer controller. Here is where I'm struggling. It just isn't coming out right ... I wasn't able to figure it out today. Tomorrow I'll make a break hopefully.
